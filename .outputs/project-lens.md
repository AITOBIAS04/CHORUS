*New Article: Argentina Built a Digital Twin of Its Citizens. It Forgot to Show Them the Code.*

Argentina just launched an AI system to simulate its population before implementing social policy — and the announcement was a disaster of typos, deepfaked ministers, and missing governance frameworks. But the question underneath is real: every computing revolution, from Hollerith's 1880 census tabulator (which became IBM) to Gallup's polling to silicon sampling, has been driven by the need to understand a population faster than it changes. MiroShark is the open-source answer to the same question Argentina is asking behind closed doors — a simulation engine where every agent, every interaction, and every confidence metric is inspectable. The census you can hold up to the light.
