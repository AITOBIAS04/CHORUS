*New Article: Stripe Compressed Payments to Seven Lines of Code. Opinion Research Is Still Stuck at Fifteen Thousand Dollars.*

Before Stripe, accepting a credit card online required weeks of bank paperwork, gateway contracts, and compliance audits. Stripe compressed it to seven lines of code and built a $107B company. The market research industry faces the same structural problem — a single focus group study costs $15,000–$50,000 and takes 4–8 weeks. MiroShark compresses the question-to-answer chain to four CLI commands and $1, exposing 41 queryable API surfaces instead of a summary PDF. The article traces the parallel from Stripe's abstraction layer to MiroShark's model slot architecture, and asks what happens when the cost floor drops far enough to change what the tool is for.
